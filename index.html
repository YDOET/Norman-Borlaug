Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr. Norman Borlaug, third from the left, trains biologist in Mexico on how to increase wheat yield-part of his life long War on hunger

Here is a timeline of Dr. Borlauf's life

  • 1914 - Born in cresco, Iowa.
  • 1933 - Leaves his faily farm to attend the university of minnesota, thanks
    to a depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration."
  • 1935 - Has to stop school and saves up more money.Works in the Civilian
    Conservation Corps, helping starving Americans."I saw how food changed
    them", he said "All of this scars on me."
  • 1937 - Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service.
  • 1938 - Marries wife of 69 years Magret Gibson. Get laid off due to budget
    cuts. Inspired over Elvin charles Stakman, he returns to school study under
    Stakman, who teaches him about breeding pest-resistant plants.
  • 1941 - Tries to enroll in the millitary after the Pearl Harbor attack, but is
    rejected. Instead, the millitary asked his lab to work on waterproof glue,DDT
    to control malaria, disinfectants, and other applied science.
  • 1942 - Recieves a ph.D in Genetics and Plant Pathology.
  • 1944 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from Dupont, leaves behind his
    pregnant wife, and flies to Mexico to head a new plant Pathology program.
    Over the next 16 years, his team breeds 6,000 different strains of diseases
    resistent wheat-including different varieties of each major climate on Earth.
  • 1945-Discovers a way to grow wheat twice each season, doubling what yields.
  • 1953-crosses a short, sturdy dwarf breed of wheat with a high yielding
    American breed, creating a strain that responds well to fertilizers. it goes on to
    provide 95% percent of Mexico's wheat.
  • 1962-Visits Delhi and brings his high-yeilding strains of wheat to the indian
    subconttinent in time to help miltigate mass starvation due to rapidly
    expanding population.
  • 1970-recieves the Nobek Peace Price
  • 1983- Help seven Africandraatically increase their maize and
    sorghume yields
  • 1984- becomes a distinguished professor at Texas A&M University
  • 2005- states "we will have to double the food supply by 2050." Argues
    that geneticallymodified crops are the only way we can meet the demand, as
    we run out of arable land. Says tha GM crops are not inherently dangerous
    because "we have been genetically modifying plants and animals for a long
    time. Long before we call it science, people were selecting the best
    breeds."
  • 2009- dies at age of 95
    "Borlaug's life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution
    that one man's lowering intellect, persistence and scientific vision can make
    to human peace and progress."
    Indian prime minister Manmohan Singh
